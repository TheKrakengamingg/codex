Use this skill for content generation, YouTube Shorts scripts, captions, hooks, descriptions, hashtags, ElevenLabs settings, voice/persona variation, or reducing repetitive AI output.

Brand voice:
- Direct, energetic, gaming-native, but not childish.
- Strong first sentence hook.
- Short punchy sentences.
- Avoid robotic phrases like "In today's video" or "Get ready to dive into".
- Prefer concrete names, stakes, and why the viewer should care.
- Do not overuse the same structure across videos.

Shorts script structure:
1. Hook: surprising claim or question in the first 1-2 seconds.
2. Context: what happened and who is involved.
3. Twist/stakes: why this matters.
4. Payoff: consequence, prediction, or call to watch for updates.

Randomization rules:
- Rotate hooks: question, warning, reveal, countdown, controversy, prediction.
- Rotate pacing: 4, 5, or 6 scenes.
- Rotate visual style only within the user's chosen art style.
- Rotate ElevenLabs voices and voice settings within safe ranges.
- Avoid repeating the same topic or intro phrase in consecutive outputs.

ElevenLabs guidance:
- Keep voiceover clear above music.
- Use premium voice rotation for variety.
- For energetic gaming content, prefer moderate stability and strong similarity boost.
- Do not fallback to fake/test audio when premium TTS fails; fail clearly.

SEO tags:
- Generate 3-5 tags specific to the story.
- Include `#shorts`.
- Prefer specific tags over generic tags when possible.
- Avoid unrelated trend tags.

Quality checks:
- Read script aloud mentally: if it sounds robotic, rewrite.
- Ensure script target word count matches requested duration.
- Ensure image prompts contain concrete visual subjects and no on-screen text.
